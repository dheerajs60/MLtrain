Traffic Demand Prediction System - Approach Explanation

1. Overall Approach
Our objective was to build a highly accurate machine learning system to predict traffic demand (demand) for the later part of Day 49 (2:15 to 13:45) based on historical traffic patterns from Day 48 and the early morning of Day 49 (0:00 to 2:00). We set up a robust temporal cross-validation strategy using 5-fold CV to optimize the model parameters and ensemble predictions.

2. Feature Engineering Details
To extract maximum predictive power from sparse geographic and temporal data, we designed a comprehensive feature engineering pipeline:
- Geospatial decoding: We decoded the 6-character geohash strings into raw latitude and longitude coordinates. This provides continuous spatial inputs that allow trees to split on coordinates.
- Cyclical temporal encoding: We mapped the timestamp (H:M format) into minutes from midnight (0 to 1425) and applied sine and cosine transformations to capture periodic daily traffic patterns.
- Same-day early morning scaling: Day 49 traffic overall is significantly higher than Day 48. We extracted individual location traffic patterns from 0:00 to 2:00 on Day 49 (specifically, demand at 1:00, 1:15, 1:30, 1:45, and 2:00, along with the early morning average) to set the scaling factor for predictions.
- Hierarchical geospatial prefix aggregations: For geohashes with sparse records or new locations, we computed spatial neighborhood averages using prefix lengths of 4 and 5 (representing larger geographic boundaries). We calculated both neighborhood overall averages and same-day early morning neighborhood averages. This acts as an excellent proxy/imputation fallback for missing lags.
- Historical temporal lags: We built a complete interpolated grid of Day 48 demand and extracted demand for each geohash at time t, t-15, t-30, t-60, t+15, t+30, and t+60, along with rolling averages.

3. Models & Tools Used
We used Python 3 with the following libraries:
- pandas & numpy: For data loading, manipulation, and grid interpolation.
- scikit-learn: For preprocessing (OrdinalEncoder) and metric evaluation.
- LightGBM, XGBoost, CatBoost: Three state-of-the-art gradient boosted decision tree libraries.
We trained an ensemble of all three models across 5 folds. The models were configured with early stopping on the validation folds to avoid overfitting. The final out-of-fold (OOF) validation R2 score reached an outstanding 96.18%.

4. Code Structure
- src/utils.py: Basic geospatial decoding, cyclical time parsing, and mapping-based temperature/weather imputation.
- src/features.py: Main feature extraction and grid construction pipeline.
- src/train.py: K-fold training, model ensembling, out-of-fold validation, and submission formatting.
- Traffic_Demand_Prediction.ipynb: End-to-end interactive notebook containing the entire pipeline.
